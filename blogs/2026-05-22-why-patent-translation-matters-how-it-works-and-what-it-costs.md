Patent translation is the conversion of patent documents from one language to another while preserving precise legal and technical meaning and the stakes of getting it wrong range from rejection to complete loss of IP enforceability in a target jurisdiction. The scale of the challenge is growing: according to WIPO's World Intellectual Property Indicators (2025), global patent applications climbed 4.9% to 3.7 million in 2024, the fastest year-on-year growth since 2018, meaning more businesses than ever are navigating multi-jurisdiction filing requirements. The content that follows will cover what patent translation involves, what qualifications to look for in a provider, and how Smartling's secure translation management system handles patents and related legal documents at scale.
